Discretisation of Linear Systems

Feb 11, 2016

We consider various methods of discretising the linear system

\begin{equation} \label{eq:1} \dot x = \begin{pmatrix} \rho & 1 \\[0em] -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}x, \end{equation}

where $\rho<0$ is a dissipation term. Our goal is to analyse the effect the choice of the discretisation scheme has on the shape and size of the invariant set we obtain for the discretised system $x^+=Ax$. We can explicitly calculate $A$ for the explicit Euler method, the implicit Euler method and the simplectic integrator scheme, furthermore we can explicitly evaluate the exact solution by evaluating the matrix exponential. For a stepsize $h$ we obtain the following discrete time systems

\begin{equation} x_e[k+1] = \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} 1+h\rho & h \\[0em] -h & 1 \end{pmatrix}}_{A_e}x_e[k] \end{equation}

for the explicit Euler method,

\begin{equation} x_i[k+1] =\underbrace{\left( \begin{array}{cc} 1-h\rho & -h \\[0em] h & 1 \end{array}\right)^{-1}}_{A_i} x_i[k] \end{equation}

for the implicit Euler method and

\begin{equation} x_s[k+1] = \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\[0em] 1+h\rho & -h \end{array}\right)^{-1} \left(\begin{array}{cc} -h & 1 \\[0em] 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)}_{A_s} x_s[k] \end{equation}

for the simplectic integration method, in addition we use the exact solution $x[k+1] = x((k+1)h) = e^{Ah}x_0$ for comparison.
